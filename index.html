Literature Survey

AI Assistant for Enhancing Employee Well-being and Office Experience

Function 01 : The Head Posture Monitoring and Emotion-Based Activity Recommendation


The Head Posture Monitoring and Emotion-Based Activity Recommendation system uses AI and webcam technology to track employees’ head posture and facial expressions, identifying signs of fatigue, stress, or low engagement. When such signs are detected, the system provides personalized recommendations—such as short breaks, relaxation exercises, or stretches—to help employees regain focus and maintain both physical and emotional well-being, ultimately enhancing productivity and workplace satisfaction.

Function 02 : Emotion Recognition through voice Analyzing


This system analyzes employee emotions by processing voice recordings, focusing on both content and frequency to ensure accurate detection. Using advanced speech processing techniques, the system identifies emotional stages and levels separately, then evaluates the overall emotional state. This enables organizations to monitor employee well-being, enhance engagement, and provide personalized support for improved productivity.

Function 03: Task Performance Prediction & Depression Level Probability Measurement uses xAI


Employee mental health strongly affects productivity, yet most workplaces still rely on basic performance tracking without addressing psychological well-being. Traditional tools like the ICD-10 depression checklist are rarely used in office settings. Recent AI studies show promise in detecting stress and depression through speech, sensors, and social media, but most lack transparency and real-world use. Using Explainable AI (XAI) methods like SHAP can make predictions more understandable and trustworthy. This research proposes combining task performance data with ICD-10-based depression assessment using a Random Forest model and SHAP explainability to create a clear, ethical, and effective workplace mental health monitoring system.

Research Gaps

Existing solutions rarely combine physical and emotional monitoring in real time. Your research creates an AI system that integrates posture, emotion, and performance to improve employee well-being.

Monitors head posture and facial expressions to detect stress or fatigue and suggests personalized activities.

Current systems for posture monitoring and emotional state detection may not effectively capture subtle signs of fatigue or inattention. There's a need for more accurate, real-time analysis of posture and emotional levels, along with better personalization to recommend activities that truly align with the employee’s emotional state.Enhancing these capabilities can improve the system’s responsiveness and overall effectiveness.

Detects emotional states by analyzing speech content and vocal patterns.

Most current emotion detection systems focus only on either vocal frequency or speech content, missing subtle emotional cues. Additionally, most are designed for English, with no support for Sinhala. A more accurate approach would analyze frequency and content separately, then combine the results. Supporting Sinhala voice input would also improve system responsiveness, allowing continuous monitoring and better support for employee productivity and well-being.

Predicts task performance and depression risk using machine learning and explainable AI.

Most workplace systems track productivity metrics like attendance or task completion but ignore employee mental health, delaying stress or depression detection. Clinical tools like ICD-10 exist but are rarely used at work, and AI-based models often act as black boxes, limiting trust. This research proposes a scalable, interpretable system combining productivity data with psychological assessments, using logistic regression and SHAP for transparency, providing an ethical and effective approach to workplace well-being.

Research Problem & Solution

From Problem to Solution: Advancing Employee's Working Environment

Problem

Employees often experience fatigue, stress, or low engagement at work, which can negatively impact their productivity, physical health, and emotional well-being. Traditional monitoring methods are either manual, intrusive, or ineffective, making it hard for organizations to identify and address these issues in real time.


Proposed Solution

Your research proposes an AI-based system that continuously monitors employees’ posture, facial expressions, and voice to detect signs of stress, fatigue, or emotional imbalance. Based on the analysis, it provides personalized activity recommendations (like breaks, stretches, or relaxation exercises) and predicts task performance and depression risk using machine learning. This approach enables proactive interventions, improving physical health, emotional well-being, and workplace productivity.

Methodology

Unveiling the Evidence: Exploring Our Research Methodology for Enhancing Employee's Working Experience

The methodology involves developing The Head Posture Monitoring and Emotion-Based Activity Recommendation component uses a webcam to capture employees’ head posture and facial expressions during work. The video data is preprocessed to detect faces and key posture points, which are then analyzed using AI models to identify signs of fatigue, stress, or low engagement. Based on this analysis, the system generates personalized recommendations such as breaks, stretches, or relaxation exercises. A feedback loop tracks the effectiveness of these activities and updates the system to improve accuracy over time.


The Voice Analysis for Emotion Detection component records employees’ voice samples during calls, meetings, or prompted sessions. The audio is preprocessed to remove noise and extract key features such as pitch, tone, and frequency. AI models then analyze these features to detect emotional states based on both speech content and frequency patterns. This voice-based emotional data is integrated with posture and facial expression analysis to provide a comprehensive understanding of the employee’s emotional state.


The Task Performance Prediction and Depression Risk Assessment component collects daily task completion data, deadlines, and productivity metrics. Machine learning models are trained to predict task performance scores, while explainable AI (xAI) techniques estimate the probability of depression and highlight contributing factors. Based on these predictions, the system can provide personalized interventions or escalate issues to management when necessary.
